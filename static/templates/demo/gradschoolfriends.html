Who did your advisor know in grad school?: crowdsourcing PhD graduation dates

OH NO I'M GETTING OLD

Michael Bernstein, Aug 22 2017

I tried a bunch of different combinations of crowdsourcing and the Semantic Scholar corpus before landing on this one. For example, normalizing failure — what were our least cited papers in grad school? Or experimenting with normalized citation counts for CHI as per the bibliometrics literature. But ultimately I went with a simple one: who knew each other in graduate school?

Here are the results, HCI edition: Who did your advisor know in grad school?

We often forget that our academic heroes were once graduate students too, and that many of them got to know each other back as young'ins. I didn't know, for example, that my Stanford colleague James Landay graduated the same year as Paul Dourish, or that the HCI superstar graduation rate started skyrocketing in the late 90's.

I created an initial set of people to tag by looking at the Semantic Scholar corpus for coauthors on at least 15 CHI, UIST, UbiComp or CSCW papers. It's a rough and inaccurate index, I know, but gave me enough to get started. I then asked workers on Daemo to search the web for these authors' curriculum vitaes or other information that would indicate when they finished their PhDs. Finally, I linked it back to the Semantic Scholar corpus to identify, for each author, the highest-cited paper they published before they finished their PhDs.

Please add yourself via the spreadsheet if you'd like!
